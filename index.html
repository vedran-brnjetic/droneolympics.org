Drone Olympics 2019

We are calling the whole drone & UAV industry to join in a new kind of drone innovation event in Helsinki, Espoo and Vihti, Finland in 2.-5. September 2019

Made possible by Ultrahack

Let's make the most out of drones!

Take a part in challenging tasks and innovate new services & ways to use various technologies for e.g. in farming, forestry, rescue services, logistics, traffic and maintenance.

Smart city mission: “Toxic Waste Problem” by City of Helsinki and Finnish Finnish Geospatial Research Institute of National Land Survey

There are waste bins from the sea on the island of Harakka, which can also be hazardous waste. The task is to map out the number and location of waste bins from the mainland, to identify any markings in the containers, and to assess what it is, document the case and, if possible, take a sample of the vessel or the water.

Read more & join the challenge

Smart city mission: “Building Care” by City of Espoo and Finnish Geospatial Research Institute of National Land Survey

Design an optimized flight path and estimate the roof top maintenance need of given properties. Properties will be marked on the map.

Read more & join the challenge

Drone mission: “In-Cloud Icing Performance Tests in the VTT Icing Wind Tunnel” by VTT

How a drone can operate in in-cloud icing conditions during wintertime?

Test A) Drone's operational performance in icing conditions

Test B) Battery tests in the cold conditions

Read more & join the challenge

MISSION: Supporting Police and Rescue Department’s onsite operative decision making

Task: Your drone team arrives at a car accident site. Your team task is to provide information concerning an occurred accident for the Police and Rescue authorities...

Read more & join the challenge

Innovation mission: “Fly high and back” by Finnish Metereological Institution

Build and operate a drone capable of reaching 13 000 m altitude, gather an air quality sample and land safely.

Bonus points will be given for the level of autonomous operation and capability to sustain operation autonomously without human intervention. Landing dock is recommended.

Read more & join the challenge

Innovation mission: “Flowering Forest” by Natural Resources Institute Finland, Luke and Finnish Geospatial Research Institute of National Land Survey, FGI

Software challenge: Count the number of purple spruce flowers and assess the condition of individual trees on one hectare testing area within a commercial seed orchard.

Read more & join the challenge

Agricultural mission: “Count the Grass” by Luke, Maatalousverkosto and Finnish Geospatial Research Institute of National Land Survey

Estimate the total amount of grass crop harvest and map the crop variation. Competitors receive harvest information from a few samples (wet weight ~ 3pcs, overall moisture estimate)

Read more & join the challenge

Innovation mission “Micro-scale Weather” by Vaisala

Utilize data from Wind lidar to create more complete model micro-scale local weather for drone operations.

Read more & join the challenge
